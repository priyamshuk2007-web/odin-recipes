Chola bhatura

Chole Bhature is a classic and popular Punjabi meal of a spicy, tangy chickpea curry (chole) paired with a soft, fluffy, deep-fried leavened bread (bhature). This hearty and indulgent combination of Chole Bhature is often enjoyed with pickles, onions, and lemon and is a popular choice for breakfast or brunch across North India.

Lets start the cooking process

  1. Use a blender to make a paste of chopped onion, tomatoes,ginger and garlic

  2. Heat 2 to 3 tablespoons oil in a pan or kadai/wok. Add the whole garam masala (spices) â€“ cumin seeds, tej patta, cinnamon, cloves, cardamoms and black peppercorns Fry it

  3. Now add the paste , the ground and dry spices and cooked chickenpea curry and stir it well.

  4. Add 1 to 1.5 cups water or add as required. Also add salt as per taste and mix well

  5. Serve chickpea curry with onion slices, lemon wedges along with bhatura. Enjoy the chickpea curry

Now let us start making the bhatura

  1. Using a sieve, sift the all-purpose flour, salt and baking soda in a mixing bowl. Then add the semolina and sugar to the sifted ingredients
  2. Now to the sifted ingredients, add the curd (yogurt) and oil. Mix very well with a spoon. Adding water in parts first mix and then knead to a smooth and soft dough and let it rest for 40 mins
  3. Take small rolls from the dough and use a rolling pin to make it flat and place it in a pan for deep frying
  4. When light golden or golden, remove the bhatura and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil. Roll and fry all bhaturas this way .
